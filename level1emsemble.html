Demonstrate Ensemble Skills in a Dance

In this standard you will learn and perform a 1-2-minute-long dance. You will be assessed on your ability to perform with others including spatial awareness, accurate timing with other dancers, and sensitivity in performer interactions.

Techniques for Achieved

To gain achieved the student must be a member of an ensemble dance that is 1-2 minutes long. The student shows some ensemble awareness but lacks clarity.

  • Has moments of support or understanding in the genre

  • Watching others for cues

  • Non-unison movement

  • Moments around other dancers lack flow

  • Focus is drawn away from what was choreographed

  • Confidence is lacking

Techniques for Merit

To gain merit the student must be a member of an ensemble dance that is 1-2 minutes long. The student shows good ensemble awareness.

  • Recalls the dance without help from others and reproduces the correct choreography

  • Usually, accurate timing

  • Smooth entrances and exits

  • Shows awareness of other performers with timing and pathways

  • Shows appropriate focus

  • Eyes are generally focused on direction or group members as planned in choreography

  • General confidence

Techniques for Excelence

To gain excellence the student must be a member of an ensemble dance that is 1-2 minutes long. The student shows excellent ensemble skills

  • Recalls and reproduces the dance as intended by choreographer

  • Maintains accurate timing

  • Smooth entrances and exits

  • Shows awareness of other performers with timing and pathways

  • Shows appropriate focus

  • Focus is always drawn to other group members or direction of travel specified in choreography

  • Confidence is seen throughout the performance
